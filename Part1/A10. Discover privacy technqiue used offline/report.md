## Secure Disposal
In the physical world, once a object of media has been destroyed it does not necessarily mean that all evidence of that information has been destoryed. Backups exist for most media created in the modern world and inadequate 
waste of media is a genuine risk of revealing personal data that may have been stored on the media. Secure techniques of dealing with unneeded includephysical destruction such as shredding of data for example hard drives 
which guarantee deletion of it because it is disintegrated into small bits incapable of being rebuilt, also another physical based disposal technique is degaussing which is the utilisation of a magnetic field to remove the 
very functioning of media (degaussing only works on hard drives or devices similar to function of hard drives). A digital based privacy technqiue used offline is data overwritting which is essentially re writing the contents
of a media to something meaningless and unrecoverable such as writing over an unneeded drive in 1's and 0's which is binary.
