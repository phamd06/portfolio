## ATMs
Automated Teller Machines are an obvious example of an object used in public space that has implemented several security concepts. Authentication is a heavily relevant security concept which ensures someone is who they are claiming to be, and ATMs enforce this through proving that the person accessing the ATM has their *bank card as their possession (or something they have)* while their *PIN number as something they know*, this is also known as Two Factor Authentication.

## Digital Locker Systems
Digital locker systems, such as parcel pickup lockers, implement key security concepts to ensure that deliveries are accessed only by authorised users. They use authentication methods like unique codes, QR scans, or mobile app verification to confirm a user’s identity, while access control ensures that only the correct individual can open a specific locker. Confidentiality is maintained by protecting personal and delivery information, often supported by encryption during data transmission between the locker and backend systems. Integrity ensures that locker assignments and access records cannot be altered or tampered with, preventing unauthorised access to parcels.

## Transperth Card
Transperth Cards are an example of how to combine minimum security while keeping incredibly fast and secure transactions. Transperth cards combine availability, integrity and authentication as security concepts heavily implemented. Authentication is shown by proving the card is valid and linked to the correct owner. Integrity is important in context to these cards as it is implemented as a system to prove the correct fare is deducted and that the balance on the card is not modified illegally. Availability is most important out of any security concepts implemented as security designers must make sure that the systems of the card must work real-time for fast easy access to public transport, but also must be made easy to handle as thousands of these cards may be used at once, making transport cards a hard system to implement both security and availability.
