## Western Sydney University Cyberattack October 2025
On October 23rd 2025, Western Sydney University released an official press release that announced the event of a cybersecurity attack that impacted the university's integral systems, leading to unauthorised access and collection of students and staffs' personal information.

This attack is a clear example of how multiple fundamental cybersecurity aspects are displayed in real life. These include:
1. Unauthorised System Access - The attacker of this event gained access to the university's systems, which leads to the clear indication of terrible security. This calls for stronger access control, perhaps larger authentication processes and system infrastructure changes
2. Third-Party Risk - One of the attacker's objectives was clearly access to personal information of students and staffs. The method in which this was accessed was through third party systems not managed by the university however was evidently connected to them. Universities across the world rely on cloud based third party services heavily and this event is a prime example that shows security around information inheritance must be strengthened.
3. Incompetent Monitoring - In the public announcement in October of 2025, the university mentions there had been numerous unusual activity occuring between June and September which had been reproted and yet no strong action had been made to initiate reporting. This insuinates stronger reporting mininums, the need for continuous security monitoring and a faster detection algorithm.

## Qilin Ransomware Attacks Four Australian Businesses
Between late January and early March 2026, the well known cyber criminal ransomware group Qilin targeted four Australian businesses across different sectors, demanding payments through the form of ransomware.

This attack is a clear example of how multiple fundamental cybersecurity aspects are displayed in real life. These include:
1. Ransomware as a Service - The Qilin group works as a ransomware-as-a-service, meaning that their ransomware is treated as a product sold to other cyber criminals to use for their own benefit. This is a major concern for cybersecurity as it means that anybody regardless of their knowledge on computer systems has a way to attack systems, increasing cyberattacks and holding businesses captive of their data. To mitigate the damage, business must absolutely create offline backups able to access at any time.
2. Compromised Credentials - In this event, all four businesses had their system defensives compromised through not typical cyber attack but purely from Qilin logging in. Many of the businesses had reused or weak passwords, no Multi Factor Authentication and outdated VPN's which left many credentials whose access to the system is critical; compromised. To fix this, it is as simple as creating stronger passwords, enabling many steps of Multi Factor Authentication and patching.
3. Lateral Movement/ Privilege Escalation - Once Qilin had entered the network through one source, it took them little time to completely take over the entire network. This is through the usage of gaining admin credentials and gaining absolute powers in the system. By doing so, they are able to launch their ransomware across the entire network through the powers of admin. To fix this issue, stronger segregation between devices and networks are required, privileges granted must be minimised.

## Victorian Department of Education Jan 2026
In early January 2026, the Victorian Department of Education had announced that a third party system used by the department and hundreds of schools had been hacked.

This attack is a clear example of how multiple fundamental cybersecurity aspects are displayed in real life. These include:
1. Supply Chain Attacks - This event is a prime example of how main systems are able to defend off attacks but are infiltrated through third party programs designed to store data for the main systems. The department of education and their systems may have been incredibly secure however when agreeing to use third party data storages, this event shows us that if those third party softwares are compromised, it means the entire system is compromised. For mitigation, its highly recommended for securty audits to ensure third party systems are able to defend off attacks, and limiting their access to your secure storage.
2. Excessive Data Access and Poor Segmentation - In this event, one single third party had stored every single piece of data in one location. This is incredibly poor data segmentation and a serious risk of data security, evidently seen by this aftermath. Data must be separated into different locations by any means, third parties should not have admin privileges and data stored in storage must be encrypted in case of attacks.
3. Accessor Authentication - Though obvious the attacker should not have gained access to the third party's data in the first place, it is still unsatisfactory that the vendor did not have any authentication processes in place to properly identify if the accessor is authorised. Automatic alerts for large data downloads, consistent checks of accessing, multi factor authentication and storing audit logs are some ways to mitigate this issue.
