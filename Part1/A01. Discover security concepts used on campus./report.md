## Security Cameras:
Security cameras are placed around all of campus to monitor real time activites, providing safety, deterrence and secureness for all persons on campus. All sorts of criminal activities which if committed under surveillance can be immediately recorded and made playback for evidence of such crime. Security cameras are an example implementation of security monitoring, deterrence, evidence collection and non repudiation.

## Unifi Wifi:
There is available free wifi on campus for students and members of the faculty however this wifi is heavily secured and monitored real time. More importantly, the public wifi at campus (Unifi) requires user authentication which is a key concept discussed in Access Controls. Users are required to verify their identity; that they are indeed a student or member of the faculty before gaining access to the wifi. There are also separate wifi networks, of which one is guest network used for members of the public.

## Student Card:
Students have a unique student card that provides a layer of added security authentication for them which verifies they are who they are. Inbuilt in the student card also demonstrates the authorisation, showing what the student is allowed and not allowed to do. Authorisation is a security concept that is integral part of Access Control.
